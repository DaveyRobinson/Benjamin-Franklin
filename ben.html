Benjamin Franklin

A founding father of the United States

Benjamin Franklins conducts his famous kite and key experiment

Benjamin Franklin did a lot of things in his lifetime from 1706-1790. Just a few are listed below:

  • 1717: Invented a pair of fins to wear on his hands while he swam.
  • 1728: Had his first paper published titled "Articles of Belief and Acts of Religion".
  • 1729: Wrote a paper on why paper money was needed in the colonies.
  • 1736: Printed his first run of paper money for New Jersey.
  • 1737: Was appointed the first Postmaster of Philadelphia.
  • 1746: Started his first of many electrical experiments.
  • 1752: Conducts his kite and key experiment in which he flys it into a thunderstorm to help find electricity.
  • 1753: Was appointed Joint Postmaster General of North America.
  • 1776: Helped write the Declaration of Independence.
  • 1783: Invented the Bifocals.

Ther was no doubt that Benjamin Franklin was smart man. Growing up he stopped going to church to study. He became a vegetarian because it was cheaper so he could buy books. He was learning man. He was the jack of all trades.
